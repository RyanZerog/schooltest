Joe Z. Tsien

Dean of Superintelligence & Precision Health Institute

Brain Decoding and Connectome Theory

钱卓教授是国际知名的脑科学家，曾师从诺贝尔奖得主，他在遗传与基因工程领域的贡献尤为显著，开发了Cre-lox神经遗传技术及基因和蛋白敲除技术。教授在学习与记忆机制的研究中，于1999年发现了关键的基因开关NR2B，并创建了转基因聪明鼠，这一成果引起了广泛关注。

此外，钱教授还启动了大脑破译计划，提出了"连接论"和"神经自我信息论"，为理解大脑功能提供了新的理论框架。其研究成果不仅获得了多项科学奖项与荣誉，还推动了国内多个研究机构的建立，并在吸毒筛查技术与人工智能无人驾驶领域发挥了重要作用，致力于推动中国脑科学及生命科学的发展。

Professor Joe Z. Tsien is an internationally renowned neuroscientist who studied under a Nobel Prize laureate. He has made significant contributions in the fields of genetics and genetic engineering, developing the Cre-lox neural genetic technology and gene and protein knockout techniques. In his research on the mechanisms of learning and memory, he discovered the key gene switch NR2B in 1999 and created transgenic "smart mice," which garnered widespread attention.

Additionally, Professor Tsien initiated the Brain Decoding Project, proposing the "connectome theory" and "neural self-information theory," providing a new theoretical framework for understanding brain function. His research achievements have received numerous scientific awards and honors, facilitated the establishment of several research institutions in China, and played a vital role in drug screening technologies and artificial intelligence in autonomous driving, contributing to the advancement of neuroscience and life sciences in China.

Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) and Neural Signal Processing

脑机接口（BCI）领域正处于快速发展的阶段，成为神经科学和临床医学研究的前沿。特别是我国将其纳入十三五规划的科技创新重点项目之一，显示出对该领域深远的战略重视。通过信号获取与分析、软件工具开发、侵入性手术技术创新和临床应用的综合方法，深入探讨大脑功能机制，并设计新型临床工具以改善神经系统疾病的诊断与治疗效果。

我们将利用脑皮层电图信号进行系统的神经科学研究，结合解剖学与行为测量，分析复杂数据集。同时，采用C++、Matlab和Python等编程语言开发BCI2000等软件工具，作为全球脑机接口研究的标准，并融入机器学习与信号处理技术以优化数据分析流程。此外，我们还将开发新型脑图谱技术，提高侵入性脑外科手术的安全性与有效性，并积极推动研究成果的商业化，以促进神经外科实践的转变，最终改善患者的治疗体验与效果。

通过本研究，我们希望不仅能在基础科学层面上获得突破性进展，还能在临床应用上实现重大创新，从而改变人们对大脑及其功能的认知，为未来的神经科学研究和神经外科实践提供新的动力。

The field of Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) is rapidly evolving, becoming a frontier in neuroscience and clinical medical research. Notably, it has been incorporated into China's 13th Five-Year Plan as a key project for technological innovation, highlighting the strategic importance placed on this area. This research aims to comprehensively explore the mechanisms of brain function and design new clinical tools to improve the diagnosis and treatment of neurological disorders through a combined approach involving signal acquisition and analysis, software tool development, innovative invasive surgical techniques, and clinical applications.

We will utilize electrocorticography (ECoG) signals for systematic neuroscience research, integrating anatomical and behavioral measurements to analyze complex datasets. Moreover, we will develop BCI2000 and other software tools using programming languages such as C++, Matlab, and Python, establishing them as global standards for BCI research while incorporating machine learning and signal processing techniques to optimize data analysis workflows. Additionally, we aim to create novel brain mapping technologies to enhance the safety and effectiveness of invasive neurosurgery and actively promote the commercialization of research results to transform neurosurgical practices, ultimately improving patient treatment experiences and outcomes.

Through this research, we hope to achieve not only groundbreaking advancements in basic science but also significant innovations in clinical applications, fundamentally altering the understanding of the brain and its functions, and providing new impetus for future neuroscience research and neurosurgical practices.

Diabetes, Cancer Research and Biomarkers

佘金雄教授的研究主要集中在糖尿病、癌症以及相关生物标志物的探索与应用上。他在1型糖尿病的环境诱导机制和生物标志物的识别方面进行了深入研究，推动了科学界对糖尿病的理解和预防工作。通过高通量糖缀合物芯片技术，教授致力于开发新兴检测平台，以便快速、精准地分析糖结合蛋白和抗体的动态变化。这项技术已成为研究糖尿病及其并发症的重要工具。

此外，教授在癌症领域的研究也同样卓著，开发了针对癌症的单克隆抗体和新型免疫治疗策略，旨在提升癌症患者的治疗效果。神经科学和癌症治疗的交叉点上，佘教授的研究探索了糖尿病与癌症之间的潜在联系，揭示了代谢综合征对肿瘤进展的影响。通过跨学科的整合，佘金雄教授不仅推动了基础科研的发展，还致力于将研究成果转化为临床应用，改善患者的健康状况和生活质量。

Professor Jin-Xiong She focuses his research mainly on the exploration and application of diabetes, cancer, and related biomarkers. He has conducted in-depth studies on the environmental mechanisms that induce type 1 diabetes and the identification of biomarkers, advancing the scientific community's understanding and prevention efforts regarding diabetes. Through high-throughput glycan microarray technology, he aims to develop emerging detection platforms for the rapid and precise analysis of dynamic changes in glycoproteins and antibodies. This technology has become an important tool for studying diabetes and its complications.

In addition, Professor She has made remarkable contributions to cancer research, developing monoclonal antibodies and novel immunotherapy strategies aimed at improving treatment outcomes for cancer patients. At the intersection of neuroscience and cancer treatment, his research explores the potential links between diabetes and cancer, revealing how metabolic syndrome influences tumor progression. By integrating interdisciplinary approaches, Professor Jin-Xiong She not only advances fundamental research but also focuses on translating research findings into clinical applications, thereby improving patient health and quality of life.
