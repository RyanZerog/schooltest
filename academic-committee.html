Our academic committee consists of world-renowned experts who are leaders in their respective fields, providing strategic guidance and expertise to our research initiatives.

Joe Z. Tsien

Dean of Superintelligence & Precision Health Institute
Neuroscience Genetic Engineering Learning & Memory Intelligence Mechanisms

Education: Columbia University, MIT

Former Position: Princeton University

Recognition: Science Top 10 Breakthrough

Professor Joe Z. Tsien is an internationally renowned neuroscientist. Since 1990, he has studied under two Nobel laureates at Columbia University and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He pioneered the Cre-lox neurogenetic technology, as well as inducible and brain circuit- and cell-specific gene and protein knockout techniques, which are now widely used in laboratories around the world.

Professor Joe Z. Tsien is an authority in the field of brain science, particularly in the mechanisms of learning and memory, neural networks, and the principles of intelligence. In 1997, he became the first Chinese professor in the history of the Department of Molecular Biology at Princeton University. In 1999, Professor Qian discovered the gene switch (NR2B) that enhances learning and memory and created the world's first transgenic "smart mouse," named "Duqi." This achievement was published in Nature and was named one of the top ten scientific breakthroughs of the year by Science magazine. It was also praised by Time magazine and The New York Times, with Professor Qian being recognized as the "father of the smart mouse."

In 2007, Professor Joe Z. Tsien pioneered the "Brain Decoding Project" and served as its principal investigator. This project later inspired similar brain initiatives in Europe and the United States. Under his leadership, the Brain Decoding Team made original breakthroughs in understanding the fundamental mechanisms of brain function. In 2015, Professor Qian proposed the "Connectomics Theory" on brain design and computational principles, which has been experimentally validated across various animal brains and in over a dozen neural circuits. Later, in 2017, he introduced the "Neural Self-Information Theory," focusing on how neural pulses encode perception, cognitive learning, and decision-making in real time, which was experimentally confirmed in 2018. Since its publication in 2016, the academic papers on brain connectomics have been read more than 180,000 times. In January 2019, Forbes magazine recognized it as providing a theoretical framework for the development of artificial general intelligence and brain-like chips, with significant practical guidance.

Gerwin Schalk, Ph.D.

Committee Member
Brain-Computer Interfaces BCI2000 Brain Mapping Neurosurgery

Ph.D.: Computer & Systems Engineering

Publications: 132 peer-reviewed papers

Citations: 27,000+, H-index: 67

Dr. Gerwin is a world-renowned scholar in the field of brain-computer interfaces (BCI), ranked among the top five globally. He has made outstanding contributions to neuroscience research. He holds a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from Graz University of Technology, a Master's degree in Information Technology from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, and a Ph.D. in Computer and Systems Engineering.

His development of the BCI2000 software has become the global standard in brain-computer interface research, laying the foundation for over 2,400 peer-reviewed papers. Additionally, he successfully developed the first novel brain mapping technology for invasive neurosurgical procedures.

In terms of academic achievements, Dr. Gerwin has published 132 peer-reviewed papers in world-renowned journals such as Nature Neuroscience and Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences. His works have been cited 27,000 times, with an H-index of 67 (well above the average level of Nobel laureates), and he holds 10 patents. His research is transforming neurosurgical practices worldwide and has made significant contributions to the development of brain-computer interface technology, making him one of the most influential scientists in the field.

Jin-Xiong She, Ph.D.

Committee Member
Genetics Genomic Medicine Precision Medicine Anti-aging Research

Company: Jinfiniti Precision Medicine

Publications: 400+ articles

Citations: 18,000+, H-index: 76

Professor Jin-Xiong She is an internationally renowned expert in genetics and genomic medicine. After being forced to leave the Medical College of Georgia in 2021 due to the "China Action Plan," he has continued his research on aging mechanisms and anti-aging solutions at the precision medicine company he founded, Jinfiniti Precision Medicine, Inc., where he serves as the founder and CEO (since 2016).

In academia, he held several important positions at the Medical College of Georgia, including founding director of the Biotechnology and Genomic Medicine Center (2002-2021), chair of the Department of Pathology (2002-2017), and professor of Obstetrics and Gynecology (2017-2021). He was also an esteemed scholar at the Georgia Research Alliance (GRA).

Professor She led the establishment of the genomics "public facility platform" (2002-2021) and the proteomics and mass spectrometry "public facility platform" (2003-2021), driving the development of interdisciplinary technological platforms. Prior to this, he held key positions at the University of Florida College of Medicine (1991-2002), including assistant professor, associate professor, and full professor in the Department of Pathology, director of the Mammalian Genetics Center's DNA Sequencing "Public Facility Platform," and director of the NIDDK Biotechnology Center, establishing his academic reputation in the fields of diabetes and immunogenetics.

Professor She has published over 400 academic articles, with more than 18,000 citations. His Google H-index is 76, and his i10-index is 240. He is one of the 20 most influential scientists in Georgia, USA, and serves as the editor-in-chief or editorial board member for several renowned international academic journals. He has also served as president of the American Chinese Society of Genetics and the chair of the Immunology and Immunogenetics Section of the American Diabetes Association.
